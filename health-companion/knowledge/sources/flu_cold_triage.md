# Common Cold & Flu Triage
**Source:** CDC
**Type:** Symptom Management

## Influenza (Flu) vs. Common Cold

| Symptom | Cold | Flu |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Gradual | **Abrupt** (Hits typically within 3-6 hours) |
| **Fever** | Rare | **Common** (often high, 100-102째F / 37.8-39째C) |
| **Aches** | Slight | **Severe** (Muscle/body aches) |
| **Chills** | Uncommon | **Fairly Common** |
| **Fatigue** | Sometimes | **Common** (can last weeks) |
| **Sneezing** | Common | Sometimes |
| **Chest Discomfort** | Mild | Common (can become severe) |
| **Headache** | Rare | Common |

## Home Management (Self-Care)
*   **Rest:** Essential for recovery.
*   **Hydration:** Water, broth, electrolyte drinks to prevent dehydration.
*   **Fever Control:** Acetaminophen (Tylenol) or Ibuprofen (Advil) as directed. *Note: Avoid aspirin in children.*
*   **Humidifier:** Helps with congestion.

## When to Seek Medical Care (Red Flags)

### Adults
1.  **Difficulty Breathing:** Shortness of breath or rapid breathing.
2.  **Persistent Pain:** Pressure in chest or abdomen.
3.  **Dizziness:** Sudden dizziness or confusion.
4.  **Severe Vomiting:** Persistent vomiting.
5.  **Recurrence:** Flu symptoms improve but then return with fever and worse cough.

### Children
1.  **Fast breathing** or trouble breathing.
2.  **Bluish lips** or face.
3.  **Ribs pulling in** with each breath.
4.  **Chest pain.**
5.  **Dehydration:** No urine for 8 hours, dry mouth, no tears.
6.  **Alertness:** Not waking up or interacting.
7.  **Fever:**
    *   Any fever in infants < 12 weeks.
    *   Fever > 104째F (40째C) in any child.
